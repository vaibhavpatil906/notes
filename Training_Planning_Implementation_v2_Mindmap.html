Training Basics

Training Coordinator — Role

  • Creates training schedules; tracks outcomes; maintains records
  • Trains new hires on company policies; uses best training methods
  • Gathers feedback from employees/management; identifies weaknesses
  • Attends seminars to learn new techniques; coordinates future sessions
  • Recruits & trains new Trainers; manages training budget
Types of Training — By Structure

Structured

Designed by training institution in advance; covered within stipulated time; planned via expert group meetings

Semi-structured

Curriculum may change to suit trainee needs mid-program; trainees react when training gains momentum

Unstructured

Trainees structure schedule with trainer; objective must be clear; only for manageable groups

9-Step Curriculum Development
  1. Identification of training needs
  2. Defining learning objectives — SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  3. Selection of training methods — Lecture, Discussion, Seminar, Conference
  4. Design curriculum content — core content & learning materials
  5. Creation of learning resources — textbooks, handouts, slideshows, videos, online modules
  6. Design learning activities — group discussions, case studies, role-playing, hands-on projects
  7. Development of assessment methods
  8. Pilot test — trial run with small group; identify issues
  9. Finalization & Implementation
Key Pedagogical Approaches

Problem-Based Learning (PBL)

Engage actively with meaningful problems. Collaborative; self-directed. No single defined solution.

Constructivism

Learner actively constructs own knowledge. Uses prior knowledge as foundation. Learner = active cognizing subject.

Flipped Classroom

Readings at home; problem-solving in class. Students watch online lectures at home; engage with mentor in class.

Programmed Learning Material
  • Material presented in small steps called frames
  • Each frame: small info + question/activity calling for overt response
  • Learner informed if response correct/incorrect before next frame
  • Frames prepared so 95% responses are correct
  • Immediate feedback and reinforcement
CAI (Computer-Assisted Instruction) Modes

Drill & Practice

Computer presents exercises; feedback = congratulatory or corrective; learner-controlled pace.

Tutorial

Info in small bits followed by questions; computer analyses response; appropriate feedback given.

Simulation

Real-life systems/phenomena — Gaseous Laws, Nitrogen Cycle, Ecological system.

Discovery

Inductive approach — problems presented; learner solves via trial and error.

Gaming

Teaching through play or game; boosts engagement.

Inductive vs Deductive

Inductive: Specific examples → general principles
Deductive: General principles → specific applications

Types of Entrepreneurship

Opportunity-based

Self-initiated; innovative; creative thinking; real entrepreneurship.

Necessity-based

Career as last resort due to family circumstances or past job disappointment; compulsion not choice.

Technological

Introduces new technology. Ex: solar-dried fish (new process, existing product).

Geographical

Distributes across regions. Must analyze consumer demand. Ex: Kerala fish sold in UP.

First Aid

Purpose of First Aid (6)

  • Preserve life
  • Promote recovery
  • Prevent further injury
  • Prevent complications
  • Maintain comfort
  • Help reach medical care

Burns — 3 Types

  • Superficial — outer epidermis only
  • Partial thickness — very painful; destroys epidermis; fatal if >20% body
  • Full thickness — NOT painful ⚠ — misleads first-aider; needs immediate attention
Burn First Aid — Key Steps
Cool with cold water ≥10 minutes → Remove rings/jewelry → Cover with sterile dressing → Do NOT cover face burns → Keep cooling till doctor arrives
RICE — Strains/Sprains/Contusions
RICE
Rest (stop using injured part) → Ice (max 20 min/hr; contracts blood vessels) → Compression (elasticised bandage) → Elevation (above heart level)
Strain = muscle/tendon | Sprain = ligament | Contusion = bruise from direct blow
CPR — DRSABCD
Steps
Danger → Response (ask name, squeeze shoulders) → Send for help → Airway (open mouth) → Breathing → CPR (compressions + rescue breathing) → Defibrillator
Dressings & Bandages

Types of Dressing

  • Gauze — most common; loosely woven cotton; air circulation
  • Adhesive (Band-Aid) — minor cuts/scrapes; adhesive backing
  • Hydrocolloid — gel-forming; waterproof; shallow wounds
  • Foam — cushioning; ulcers, burns, surgical wounds
  • Alginate — seaweed-derived; heavily exuding wounds
  • Transparent Film — waterproof; easy monitoring without removal

Types of Bandage

  • Crepe/Roller — versatile; holds dressing, compression, joint
  • Compression — stretchable; soft tissue injury
  • Snake Bite — special tension indicator markings
  • Triangular — right-angle triangle; slings, limb support
  • Tubular — elasticated circle; arms/legs/wrists/ankles
  • Cohesive — sticks to itself NOT to skin

Continuous Professional Development (CPD)

Ongoing process by which teachers acquire, develop and strengthen skills to become more effective. Responds to ever-changing professional environment.

  • Updating knowledge
  • Improving classroom practices
  • Dealing with emerging challenges
  • Professional networking — ICT-based
Microteaching
  • Introduced by Dr. Dwight W. Allen, Stanford University, 1960s
  • Mini-lesson: 10–15 minutes Ans: C | Pupils: 5–7
  • Video recorded; self-reflection + peer feedback
  • Components: teacher, pupils (5–7), brief lesson, objectives, feedback (audio/video/CCTV)
9-Step Microteaching Procedure
  1. Define skill — in terms of teaching behaviors
  2. Demonstrate skill — by experts or via videotape
  3. Plan the lesson — with supervisor's help
  4. Teach the lesson — small group 5–10 pupils; observed/videotaped
  5. Discussion — feedback provided to trainee
  6. Re-plan — revise lesson based on feedback/suggestions
  7. Re-teach — revised lesson to another small group (same class)
  8. Re-discuss — feedback again; encouraging suggestions
  9. Repeat — 'teach–reteach' cycle until desired skill level achieved

  • Standardization + local adaptation — balance required globally
  • E-learning, VR, AR enable remote/interactive training for dispersed teams
  • Digital literacy, language proficiency, cross-cultural communication skills needed
  • Continuous learning/lifelong development now emphasized
  • Outsourcing of training to specialized firms globally
