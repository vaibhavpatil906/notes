◈ INTERACTIVE MIND MAP — THE APPRENTICES ACT, 1961
§01 Overview & Timeline
1961
12 December 1961 — Act passed by Parliament. Purpose: Regulate & control training of apprentices in industry.
1962
1 March 1962 — Act came into force across the whole of India (including Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh).
1973 · 1986
First and Second amendments — foundational structural changes to the apprenticeship framework.
1997 · 2007
Third and Fourth amendments — expansion of categories including graduate and technician apprentices.
2014
Fifth amendment (most recent) — Major overhaul: introduced optional trades, portal-based registration, employer flexibility.
2018
Maharashtra State Amendment — Stipend rates, 2.5%–25% workforce mandate, compensation rules revised for Maharashtra.
Objective of the Act: (a) To regulate and promote training of apprentices in industry. (b) To utilize industry facilities for practical training to meet skilled manpower requirements. (Objectives 'a' and 'd' per MPSC 2023)
§02 Key Definitions (Section 2)
§2

Apprentice

A person undergoing apprenticeship training in pursuance of a contract of apprenticeship.
§2

Trade Apprentice

An apprentice who undergoes apprenticeship training in any designated trade.
§2

Graduate / Technician Apprentice

Holds or is pursuing a degree or diploma in engineering/non-engineering/technology from a Government-recognised institution and undergoes training in a designated trade.
§2

Technician (Vocational) Apprentice

Holds or is pursuing a vocational certificate (2-year course) after secondary school recognised by All-India Council; undergoes training in a designated trade.
§2

Designated Trade

Any trade/occupation or subject field in engineering/non-engineering/technology/vocational course specified by Central Government after consulting Central Apprenticeship Council via Official Gazette.
§2

Optional Trade

Any trade/occupation/subject field determined by the employer for the purposes of this Act (not prescribed by Central Government).
§2

National Council (NCVT)

Central body set up in 1956 as NCTVT; renamed NCVT in 1981. Oversees vocational training nationwide.
§2

Regional Board

Board of Apprenticeship Training registered under Societies Registration Act, 1860 — grants certificates to graduate/technician apprentices (BOAT).
§03 Training Provisions
§3

Qualifications to be an Apprentice

  • Minimum age: 14 years
  • Hazardous industries: 18 years
  • Must satisfy prescribed education & physical fitness standards
§3A/3B

Reservation of Training Places

  • SC/ST: Seats reserved as prescribed per state population ratio
  • OBC: Seats reserved as prescribed per state OBC population
  • Multi-trade: calculated on total apprentices across all trades
§4

Contract of Apprenticeship

  • Minor apprentice: guardian must sign
  • Send to Apprenticeship Adviser: within 30 days
  • Via portal (when available): within 7 days
  • Objection communicated: within 15 days
  • Registration of contract: within 30 days
§5 / 5A / 5B

Special Provisions

  • Novation (§5): Transfer to new employer with Apprenticeship Adviser approval; old contract ends
  • Optional Trade (§5A): Qualifications, tests, certificates as prescribed
  • Cross-State (§5B): Employer may engage apprentices from other states
§7

Termination of Contract

  • Auto-terminates on expiry of training period
  • Either party may apply to Apprenticeship Adviser
  • Employer's fault: pays compensation (MH: 1 month stipend)
  • Apprentice's fault: repays training cost (MH: 1 month stipend)
  • Prior training counted if old contract ended by employer's fault
§8

Number of Apprentices

  • Prescribed by Central Government
  • Several employers may pool resources via approved agency
  • MH 2018: 2.5% to 25% of total workforce (incl. contract/daily-wage workers)
§9

Practical & Basic Training

  • Every employer must arrange practical training
  • Trade apprentices without prior training: must complete basic training first
  • 500+ workers: employer sets up separate basic training facility
  • Syllabus approved by Central Govt after consulting Central Apprenticeship Council
§10

Related Instruction

  • Theory instruction at cost of employer
  • Time in theory class = paid work time
  • Prior-trained apprentices: reduced/modified theory
  • Graduate/Technician apprentices: employer must release for technical institution instruction

§11–18 Obligations, Rights & Status
§11

Employer's Obligations

  • Provide trade training per Act & rules
  • Assign qualified supervisor if employer is not qualified
  • Provide adequate instructional staff with prescribed qualifications
  • Provide facilities for trade test
  • Carry out obligations under the contract
§12

Apprentice's Obligations

  • Learn trade conscientiously and diligently
  • Aim to qualify as skilled craftsman before training ends
  • Attend practical and instructional classes regularly
  • Carry out lawful orders of employer & superiors
  • Fulfil contract obligations (graduate/vocational: maintain prescribed work records)
§15

Hours, Leave & Holidays

  • Hours determined by employer (within training duration norms)
  • Overtime: only with Apprenticeship Adviser's approval (in interest of training or public interest)
  • Entitled to same leave & holidays as establishment
§16–18

Compensation, Conduct & Status

  • Injury compensation (§16): Paid per Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 (with Schedule modifications)
  • Conduct (§17): Governed by rules applicable to corresponding employee category
  • Status (§18): Apprentice = Trainee, NOT a Worker. Labour laws do NOT apply.
Section 14 — Health & Safety: Factory apprentices are covered under Chapters III, IV & V of the Factories Act, 1948. Mine apprentices are covered under Chapter V of the Mines Act, 1952.
§13 Stipend / Payment to Apprentices
General Rule: Employer must pay stipend at least at the minimum prescribed rate (or rate as on 1 Jan 1970, whichever is higher). Apprentice CANNOT be paid on piece-rate; cannot be included in bonus/incentive/output-based schemes.
Year of Training Training Hours/Day Stipend Rate (Maharashtra 2018)
1st Year 8 hours 70% of minimum wages (semi-skilled workers) notified by State
2nd Year 8 hours 80% of minimum wages (semi-skilled workers) notified by State
3rd Year 8 hours 90% of minimum wages (semi-skilled workers) notified by State
Any Year ≥4 hours (part-time) 50% of normal monthly stipend rate
If the State has not fixed minimum wages for a particular trade → use the highest minimum wage for semi-skilled work fixed by the State. Prior school/institution training period is counted while deciding stipend rate.
§23–29 Authorities Under the Act
Authority Constituted By Key Role
National Council (NCVT) Govt. of India (1956) Oversee vocational training; grant trade apprentice certificates
Central Apprenticeship Council Central Govt. (Official Gazette) Policy advice; hear appeals from Apprenticeship Adviser decisions
State Apprenticeship Council State Govt. (Official Gazette) State-level policy; affiliated to Central Apprenticeship Council
All-India Council (AICTE) Central Govt. Recognises vocational courses for technician (vocational) apprentices
Regional Board (BOAT) Registered under Societies Act 1860 Assess and certify graduate/technician/vocational apprentices
Central Apprenticeship Adviser Central Govt. (Official Gazette) Secretary to Central Apprenticeship Council; enforce Act centrally
State Apprenticeship Adviser State Govt. (Official Gazette) Secretary to State Apprenticeship Council; enforce Act in state
Apprenticeship Advisers are Public Servants (§28) within the meaning of Section 21 of the Indian Penal Code.
Dispute Settlement (§20): Disputes → Apprenticeship Adviser → Appeal to Apprenticeship Council Committee within 30 days. Committee's decision is FINAL.
Certificate (§21): Trade apprentices passing the test → Certificate of Proficiency by NCVT. Graduate/Technician/Vocational apprentices → Certificate by BOAT. (Maharashtra: State Council of Vocational Training for state-designated trades)
§30–33 Offences & Penalties
§30(1A) — Shortfall in Apprentice Numbers
1. Employer given 1 month's notice in writing

2. If unsatisfied with explanation:
• First 3 months: ₹500 per apprentice per month shortfall
• Thereafter: ₹1,000 per month until seats are filled
§30(2) — Other Violations → ₹1,000 Each
• False or withheld information
• Refusing inspection/inquiry
• Unauthorised overtime
• Using apprentice for non-training work
• Piece-rate payment
• Including in bonus/incentive schemes
• Appointing ineligible apprentice
• Breach of contract terms
§31 — No Specific Penalty Provision
Fine: Not less than ₹1,000 and may extend up to ₹3,000
§33 — Cognizance of Offences
Courts can take cognizance only on written complaint by Apprenticeship Adviser or officer of rank Deputy Apprenticeship Adviser and above, within 6 months of the alleged offence.
§30(2A) Exception: Penalty provisions do NOT apply to establishments under the Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction (BIFR) under the Sick Industrial Companies Act, 1985.
§37 — Rules Violation Fine: Contravention of rules made under this Act may be punished with fine up to ₹50. Rules must be laid before both Houses of Parliament for 30 days.
