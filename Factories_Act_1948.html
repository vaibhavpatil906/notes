SEC 5 Public Emergency Exemption

  • Govt may exempt factory from provisions during public emergency
  • Notification in Official Gazette required
  • Max duration: 3 months at a time

SEC 7 Notice by Occupier

  • 15 days before occupying premises — written notice to Chief Inspector
  • New manager appointed → notice to Inspector within 7 days
  • No manager → occupier deemed as manager
Appeal Rule — Sec 6

If State Government or Chief Inspector refuses permission for factory registration/extension, applicant may appeal within 30 days — to Central Government (if State Govt refused) or to State Government (in any other case).

CHAPTER II

The Inspecting Staff

SEC 7A General Duties of Occupier

  • Ensure health, safety and welfare of all workers while at work
  • Obligation is "so far as is reasonably practicable"

SEC 8 Inspectors

  • Appointed by State Government by notification in Official Gazette
  • Must possess prescribed qualifications
  • Every District Magistrate shall be an Inspector for his district

SEC 9 Powers of Inspectors

Entry

Enter any place used or believed to be used as a factory

Examine

Examine premises, plant, machinery, articles or substances

Inquire

Inquire into any accident or dangerous occurrence

Documents

Require production of any prescribed register or document

Seize

Seize or take copies of register, record, or document

Health Provisions

SEC 16 Overcrowding

  • No room shall be overcrowded injuriously to health
  • Existing factories: 9.9 cubic metres per worker
  • New factories (after Act): 14.2 cubic metres per worker
  • Chief Inspector may require notice of max workers per room

SEC 18 Drinking Water

  • Points marked "drinking water" in majority language
  • Not within 6 metres of any latrine, urinal, spittoon, or drain
  • Factories with 250+ workers → cool drinking water provision in hot weather

SEC 19 Latrines & Urinals

  • Factories with 250+ workers → prescribed sanitary type required
  • Floors & walls up to 90 cm height — glazed tiles or smooth impervious surface
  • Thoroughly washed & cleaned at least every 7 days

SEC 20 Spittoons

  • Sufficient number in convenient places, kept clean
  • Spitting in factory premises → fine not exceeding ₹5
CHAPTER IV

Safety Provisions

SEC 22 Work on/near Machinery in Motion

  • Only a specially trained adult male worker wearing tight-fitting clothing (supplied by occupier)
  • Worker's name must be recorded in register
  • Belt at moving pulley: belt must be ≤ 15 cm wide
  • Women & young persons NOT allowed to clean/lubricate/adjust prime mover or transmission machinery in motion

SEC 25 Self-Acting Machines

  • Traversing part shall not run within 45 cm of any fixed structure
  • Applies to any space over which persons are liable to pass

SEC 26 Casing of New Machinery

  • Set screws, bolts, keys on revolving shafts must be sunk/encased/guarded
  • Spur, worm and toothed gearing must be completely encased
  • Violation → up to 3 months imprisonment or ₹500 fine

SEC 28 Hoists and Lifts

  • Good mechanical construction, sound material, adequate strength
  • Thoroughly examined by competent person at least once every 6 months
  • Register with prescribed particulars must be maintained

SEC 29 Lifting Machines, Chains, Ropes

  • Good construction, sound material, free from defects
  • Thoroughly examined at least once every 12 months
  • Or at intervals as Chief Inspector may specify

SEC 36 Precautions against Dangerous Fumes

  • No person allowed in confined space (tank, vat, pit, pipe) with harmful gas/fume/vapour/dust
  • Unless provided with a manhole of adequate size or other effective means of egress

SEC 36A Portable Electric Light

  • No portable electric appliance exceeding 24 volts in confined spaces without adequate safety devices
  • If inflammable gas/fume present → only flame-proof construction lamps permitted
Safety Officers — Sec 40B

Factories with 1000+ workers ordinarily employed must appoint Safety Officers. Also applicable where State Government determines manufacturing process involves risk of bodily injury, poisoning or disease.

CHAPTER IVA

Provisions Relating to Hazardous Processes

→

SEC 41A Site Appraisal Committee

  • Chairman = Chief Inspector of the State
  • Examines applications for establishment/expansion of hazardous factories
  • Recommendation to State Govt within 90 days of receipt

SEC 41B Compulsory Disclosure

  • Existing hazardous factory → inform Chief Inspector within 30 days of commencement of Amendment Act 1987
  • New hazardous process → inform within 30 days before commencing process

SEC 41C Specific Responsibilities for Hazardous Factories

Health Records

Maintain up-to-date health/medical records of workers exposed to chemical/toxic substances. Records accessible to workers.

Qualified Personnel

Appoint persons with qualifications & experience in handling hazardous substances. Dispute on qualifications → Chief Inspector's decision is final.

Medical Examination

Before assignment to hazardous job, while continuing, and after cessation — at intervals not exceeding 12 months.

SEC 41D Inquiry Committee (Central Govt)

  • Central Govt may appoint Inquiry Committee in extraordinary situations
  • Consists of Chairman + 2 other members
  • Recommendations are advisory in nature

SEC 41G Workers' Participation in Safety

  • Safety Committee required in every hazardous factory
  • Consists of equal number of representatives of workers and management
  • Promotes cooperation on safety and health

Exemption Conditions — Sec 65
  • Max work hours in any day: 12 hours
  • Spread over (including rest): 13 hours per day
  • Total hours per week (including OT): 60 hours
  • Overtime at a stretch: max 7 days
  • OT per quarter: max 75 hours
Women Workers — Sec 66
  • Work hours restricted to 6 AM to 7 PM only
  • No shift change except after a weekly or other holiday
  • Exemption possible for fish-curing/canning factories (State Govt rules, valid for max 3 years)

CHAPTER VIII

Annual Leave with Wages

SEC 79 Annual Leave — Eligibility and Rate

Eligibility

Worker must have worked for 240 days or more in a calendar year (includes lay-off days, maternity leave up to 12 weeks, and prior earned leave)

Worker TypeLeave RateCarry Forward Limit
Adult1 day for every 20 days workedMax 30 days
Child1 day for every 15 days workedMax 40 days

SEC 79 Leave Application Rules

  • Apply in writing to manager at least 15 days before leave commences
  • For public utility services: at least 30 days before
  • Leave may not be taken more than 3 times per year
  • Illness leave — granted even if application not made in time; wages paid within 15 days (30 days for public utility)

SEC 80 & 81 Wages during Leave

  • Wages at daily average of full-time earnings (excluding OT & bonus, but including DA and subsidized food value)
  • Based on days actually worked in previous month
  • Leave ≥ 4 days (adult) / 5 days (child) → wages paid in advance before leave begins
CHAPTER X

Penalties & Procedure

Sec 92 — General Offence

Occupier / Manager

2 yrs imprisonment + ₹1 lakh fine

Continued violation after conviction → daily fine up to ₹1,000. Death → min ₹25,000; Serious injury → min ₹5,000

Sec 94 — Repeat Conviction

Enhanced Penalty

3 yrs imprisonment + ₹10,000–₹2 lakh

Death repeat → min ₹35,000; Serious injury → min ₹10,000. Prior convictions older than 2 years not counted.

Sec 95 — Obstructing Inspector

Obstruction

6 months jail + ₹10,000 fine

Refusing documents, stopping workers from being examined

Sec 97 — Worker Offences

Worker Violation

Fine up to ₹500

Breaking rules or duties under Section 111

Sec 98 — False Fitness Certificate

Using Another's Certificate

2 months jail + ₹1,000 fine

Knowingly using or permitting use of another's fitness certificate

Sec 20 — Spitting

Spitting in Factory

Fine not exceeding ₹5

Spitting within factory premises
