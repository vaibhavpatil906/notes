National Occupational Standards (NOS)

Defines what a worker must DO and KNOW in a specific function
What is NOS? Core
  • Specifies performance standard an individual must achieve at work
  • Each NOS = one key function in a job role
  • Describes: Functions · Standards of Performance · Knowledge/Understanding
  • NOS do NOT describe procedures to perform a role
N · O · S Explained
  • National – Apply to whole of India; developed only by SSCs
  • Occupational – Define ALL key functions in an occupation (e.g., Mango Grower, Mason, Dialysis Technician)
  • Standards – Measurable & describe outcomes to be achieved
2 Main Purposes of NOS
  • Develop curriculum for training delivery
  • Develop assessment instruments for certification of learners
Also used for HR:
  • Workforce planning & career pathways
  • Performance appraisal systems
  • Job descriptions
Approval Path Important
SSC develops NOS
↓
NSDC QRC approval
(CEOs of SSCs + 1 NSDC rep)
↓
NSQC approval
(National Skills Qualifications Committee)

Qualification Pack (QP) & Sector Skill Council (SSC)

QP = bundle of NOS aligned to one Job Role
Qualification Pack (QP)
  • Defines set of NOS aligned to one Job Role
  • Assessment criteria created by Sector Skill Council
  • Each Performance Criteria (PC) assigned marks proportional to importance
  • Framework for training, assessments & certifications
Sector Skill Council (SSC)
  • Bridges gap between industry demand and skilling requirements
  • Operates as autonomous body (Section 8 Company or Society)
  • NSDC gives initial seed funding and incubates SSCs
Examples of SSCs:
  • Aerospace & Aviation SSC
  • Agriculture Skill Council of India
  • Automotive Skill Development Council
  • Beauty & Wellness SSC
  • Electronics Sector Skill Council of India
Mandate / Functions of SSC
  • Identify skill development needs & prepare skill catalogue
  • Develop sector skill plan & maintain skill inventory
  • Determine skills/competency standards per NSQF
  • Standardise affiliation, accreditation, exam & certification
  • Conduct skill-based assessment & certification (QP/NOS)
  • Plan & facilitate Training of Trainers (with NSDC & states)
  • Promote academies of excellence
  • Special attention: ST/SC, differently-abled, minority groups
  • Ensure trained persons get employment at decent wages
National Skill Development Fund (NSDF)
  • Set up in 2009 by Government of India
  • Raises funds from Govt & Non-Govt sectors for skill development
  • Custodian: Public Trust set up by GoI
  • Operated by Board of Trustees (Chairperson: Secretary, MSDE)
  • CEO is responsible for day-to-day administration
  • Meets objectives through NSDC

Skilling · Upskilling · Reskilling · Cross-Skilling

PM Modi's Mantra: Skilling, Reskilling and Upskilling
⬆️ Upskilling
  • Expand existing skill set
  • Enhances performance in current role
  • Advances career path
  • Ex: Marketing employees trained in digital ads & data analytics
🔄 Reskilling
  • Learning new skills outside current skillset
  • Often adjacent or entirely different path
  • Ex: Manufacturing workers reskilled to operate automated systems
Job Shadowing:
  • On-the-job training — observe another employee performing the role
  • Important reskilling technique
  • Used to onboard new employees
↔️ Cross-Skilling
  • Training in skills outside current expertise
  • Can take on multiple complementary roles
  • More flexible workforce
  • Ex: Software developers trained in multiple programming languages
🌍 Reskilling Revolution WEF
  • Initiative of World Economic Forum
  • Launched in 2020
  • Aims to empower 1 billion people with better education, skills & economic opportunity by 2030
  • India is one of the founding members
Top WEF Skills by 2030: AI & Big Data · Networks & Cybersecurity · Technological Literacy · Creative Thinking · Resilience

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)

India's largest Skill Certification Scheme
PMKVY 1.0 2015
  • Launched: 15th July 2015 (World Youth Skills Day)
  • Aim: Free short-duration training + monetary rewards for skill certification
  • Trained: 19.86 lakh candidates
Key Components:
  • Short Term Training (STT)
  • Special Projects
  • Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
PMKVY 2.0 2016-2020
  • Target: 10 million youth over 4 years
  • Trained: 1.10 crore candidates
CSCM

Implemented by NSDC
CSSM
25% funds
Implemented by SSDMs
PMKVY 3.0 2021
  • More decentralised structure
  • Greater role of District Skill Committees (DSCs)
  • Shifted from supply-based → demand-based approach
  • Trained: 7.37 lakh candidates
  • Aligned to Atmanirbhar Bharat
  • Target group: 15–45 years
PMKVY 4.0 2022–2026
  • Age group: 15–45 years
  • Educational institutions (CBSE-affiliated) set up Skill Hubs
  • Greater industry involvement in curriculum & delivery
  • Focus on AI, Machine Learning & new age skills
  • Digital platforms for enrollment, monitoring, attendance, assessment
📌 Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
  • ›Individuals with prior learning assessed & certified under RPL
  • ›Aims to align unregulated workforce competencies to NSQF
  • ›Training/orientation duration: 12–80 hours
  • ›Age group: 18–59 years
RPL 5 Stages
Stage 1 – Pre Screening (Awarding Body)
Stage 2 – Prior Learning Assessment (≥70% or <70%)
Stage 3 – Orientation (General or NOS-based, min 30 hrs)
Stage 4 – Assessment (Theory + Practical)
Stage 5 – Certification (min 70% marks)

National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF)

Outcome & Competency based · 8 Levels · Notified June 6, 2023
What is NSQF?
  • Nationally integrated education & competency-based framework
  • Organises qualifications in 8 levels (1=lowest, 8=highest)
  • Defined by learning outcomes from formal, non-formal & informal learning
  • Applicable from Class 9 onwards
  • Apex body: NSQC anchored in NCVET
  • NSQF Levels NOT directly related to study duration
History of NSQF
  • National Policy on Skill Development 2009 → recognized need for framework
  • Ministry of Labour → developed NVQF
  • Ministry of HRD → developed NVEQF
  • Both merged into NSQF
  • Originally notified: 27 Dec 2013
  • Revised & notified: 6 June 2023 (after NCrF on 10 April 2023)
  • Now managed by NCVET (formerly NSDA)

National Credit Framework (NCrF) & Academic Bank of Credits (ABC)

Meta-framework for creditization of all learning · NEP 2020 enabler
What is NCrF?
  • Developed jointly by UGC, AICTE, NCVET, NIOS, CBSE, NCERT, DGT, MoE, MSDE
  • Single credit framework: school + higher + vocational + experiential learning
  • Meta-framework (enabling framework) for NEP 2020
3 Verticals:
  • NSEQF – National School Education QF
  • NHEQF – National Higher Education QF
  • NSQF – National Skills QF

Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) Set up by UGC
  • National-level facility per NEP 2020
  • Promotes flexibility & interdisciplinary mobility across HEIs
  • Repository of all credits earned by a student
  • Credits accumulated & redeemable (within same assessment band)
  • Credits have validity/expiry per program
  • To be expanded to include school, vocational, apprenticeship, internship credits
APAAR – One Nation One Student ID
  • Full form: Automated Permanent Academic Account Registry
  • Part of 'One Nation, One Student ID' program
  • Unique 12-digit code for every student in India
  • Digitally stores degrees, diplomas, certificates, co-curricular achievements
  • Links to DigiLocker for secure document storage
  • Linked to ABC via APAAR ID → receives credits from National Academic Depository
  • Streamlines authentication for admissions & jobs
Other Credit Systems
  • CBCS (Choice Based Credit System) – by UGC; degree requires earning specified credits
  • SAMVAY (Skill Assessment Matrix for Vocational Advancement of Youth) – by AICTE; credit framework for vocational courses

Dual System of Training (DST) & On-the-Job Training (OJT)

Industry-integrated training within ITI ecosystem
Dual System of Training (DST)
  • Launched by DGT in Feb 2019
  • Effective from academic year 2019
  • Applicable in all affiliated ITIs, covers all CTS trades
  • Admission, courses, duration, exam & certification same as Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS)
  • OJT done in industry; theory covered in ITI
  • Graduates receive E-NTC (E-National Trade Certificate)
  • E-NTC holders have edge in employability over regular ITI pass-outs
Industry Eligibility (Revised):
  • Engineering Trades: min 40 employees, ₹1 Cr/year turnover
  • Non-Engineering Trades: min 6 employees, ₹10 lakh/year turnover
On-the-Job Training (OJT)
  • Practical training at workplace premises
  • Trainee placed on regular job → taught skills to perform it
  • Learns under guidance/supervision of instructor
  • Also called "Learning by Doing"
OJT Techniques:
  • Coaching – Person-to-person; identifies weaknesses; for manager-level
  • Mentoring – Juniors attached to seniors; guidance on org culture
  • Committee Assignments – Participate in decision-making
  • Job Rotation – Shift from one job to another; common for management trainees
  • Job Instruction Technique (JIT) – Step-by-step training with feedback
Off-the-Job Training Methods
Vestibule Training
Simulated workplace setup ("vestibule"). Identical equipment as real workplace. Skills practised in simulation then applied on job.
Case Method
Job situation presented on paper. Group identifies problems & offers solutions. Trainees learn from each other.
Incident Method
Trainees get outline of incident. Ask questions to gather info. Then attempt to solve the problem. (Similar to case method)
Q. OJT of students is carried out in order to: (ITI Principal 2014)
Ans: Provide opportunity to learn in real work situations

National Education Policies – Timeline

From Kothari Commission 1964 → NEP 2020
1964–66
Kothari Commission
First comprehensive commission; covered primary to higher education. Official title: "Education and National Development"
1968
NPE 1968
First National Policy on Education. Recommended: Free & compulsory education up to age 14 · 10+2+3 structure · Three language formula · 6% of national income for education
1986
NPE 1986
National System of Education (10+2+3) · Operation Blackboard · Vocational courses at +2 stage · IGNOU for distance learning · Delinking degrees from jobs
1990–91
Ramamurthy Committee
Formed to review NPE 1986. Report: "Towards an Enlightened and Humane Society". Recommended: Common school system · Work benches & practical schools for vocational education · ECCE · Right to Education as fundamental right
1992
Janardhana Reddy Committee (CABE)
Reviewed NPE implementation post-Ramamurthy report. Recommended: Navodaya Vidyalaya in each district · Vocational stream from Class 9 · Operation Blackboard to continue · District Board of Education to be set up
2020
NEP 2020
Prepared by Kasturirangan Committee (2017). Major overhaul: 5+3+3+4 structure · Vocational education from Grade 6 · 10-day bagless period (Grades 6–8) · 6% GDP spend · MHRD renamed Ministry of Education · M.Phil discontinued
NPE 1968 – Key Recommendations
  • Free & compulsory education up to age 14
  • 10+2+3 uniform structure
  • Three language formula (Regional + Hindi + English)
  • One agricultural university per state
  • Science education as high priority
  • Improve teacher status & academic freedom
  • Increase education spending to 6% national income
NEP 2020 – Key Features
  • 5+3+3+4 curricular structure
  • Vocational education from Grade 6 onwards
  • Mother tongue as medium till Grade 5 (preferably 8)
  • PARAKH – National Assessment Centre
  • GER in higher education to 50%
  • By 2025: 50% learners exposed to vocational education
  • Bachelor's of Vocation (B.Voc.) degrees continue
  • NCrF / ABC / APAAR enabled
  • MHRD → Ministry of Education
  • M.Phil discontinued
Key Institutions under NEP 2020
  • PARAKH – Performance Assessment, Review & Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development
  • NETF – National Educational Technology Forum
  • MERU – Multidisciplinary Education & Research Universities
  • NRF – National Research Foundation
  • NCIVE – National Committee for Integration of Vocational Education
  • PSSCIVE, Bhopal – Develops VET teacher training modules
  • SSSA – State School Standards Authority
