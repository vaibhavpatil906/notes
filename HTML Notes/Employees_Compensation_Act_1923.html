CHAPTER I

Preliminary — Key Definitions

SEC 2 "Employee" means

  • Any person employed in a listed category
  • Members of Indian Armed Forces are NOT covered
  • If employee dies from injury → "employee" includes his dependants who are entitled to compensation

SEC 2 "Employer" means

  • Any group/organization — registered or not
  • A managing agent (manager acting on behalf of employer)
  • Legal representative of a deceased employer
  • If employee is lent/sent on hire → the temporary employer becomes the "employer" for that period
Key Definitions — Quick Reference
Commissioner — Commissioner for Workmen's Compensation appointed under Section 20
Minor — Person who has NOT attained the age of 18 years
Employer (lent employee) — Person for whom employee is temporarily working
CHAPTER II

Workmen's Compensation — Liability

SEC 3 Employer's Liability — When Payable

When liability arises

Personal injury caused to employee by accident arising out of and in the course of employment

Exceptions — When employer is NOT liable
  • Injury does not result in total or partial disablement for a period exceeding 3 days
  • Injury (not causing death or permanent total disablement) caused when employee was under the influence of drink or drugs
  • Injury caused by willful disobedience of an order/rule framed for safety (not resulting in death/PTD)
  • Injury caused by willful removal or disregard of any safety guard/device (not resulting in death/PTD)

SEC 3(2) Occupational Diseases — Schedule III

Schedule PartJob TypeRequired Service Period
Part AJobs in Part A of Schedule IIINo minimum service period needed
Part BJobs in Part B of Schedule IIIMust have worked at least 6 months with same employer
Part CJobs in Part C of Schedule IIIMinimum continuous period specified by Central Government

If conditions are met → the occupational disease is treated as an injury caused by an accident.

SEC 4

Compensation Calculation Formula

Type of InjuryFormula / AmountMinimum Floor
⚰ Death 50% × Monthly Wages × Relevant Factor(Age-based factor from Schedule IV) ₹1,20,000 (₹1.2 Lakh)
🦽 Permanent Total Disablement 60% × Monthly Wages × Relevant Factor(Age-based factor from Schedule IV) ₹1,40,000 (₹1.4 Lakh)
🩹 Permanent Partial Disablement Schedule I Part II injury → % of PTD compensation as specified
Other injuries → % proportionate to loss of earning capacity (assessed by qualified medical practitioner)		 —
⏱ Temporary Disablement (total or partial) 25% of Monthly WagesPaid as half-monthly payments —
🪦 Funeral Expenses (additional) Employer deposits ₹2,500 with Commissioner for payment to eldest surviving dependent —

SEC 5 Method of Calculating Monthly Wages

  • 12+ months service: Total wages of last 12 months ÷ 12
  • Less than 1 month service: Average monthly wages of another employee doing same work in same area
  • All other cases: 30 × (total wages in last continuous period) ÷ (number of days in that period)

SEC 4A Default Penalty

  • Compensation must be paid as soon as it falls due
  • Non-payment within 1 month → Commissioner orders simple interest @ min 12% p.a.
  • Unreasonable delay → additional penalty up to 50% of unpaid amount
  • Partial liability accepted → provisional payment must be made to Commissioner or employee

SEC 6 Review of Half-Monthly Payments

  • Either party can request review; normally requires doctor's certificate of changed condition
  • Commissioner can continue / increase / reduce / stop the payment
  • If permanent disablement confirmed → can be converted to lump sum (deducting amounts already paid)

SEC 7 Commutation of Half-Monthly Payments

  • Half-monthly payments can be converted to lump sum by agreement
  • If parties disagree: after 6 months of continued payments, either party can apply to Commissioner for lump-sum redemption
SEC 8

Distribution of Compensation

Critical Rule on Direct Payment

  • Employer cannot pay compensation directly to family in case of death
  • Direct payment will NOT be treated as compensation
  • Must be deposited with Commissioner
  • Advance allowed: max 3 months' wages to dependants; advance deducted from final compensation

After Commissioner Receives Deposit

  • Commissioner's receipt = proof of employer's completed payment
  • Commissioner identifies dependants; may publish or personally send notices
  • If no dependant exists → money returned to employer
  • On employer's request → Commissioner must provide statement of distribution
  • Minimum ₹10 amount → may also be deposited on behalf of recipient
SEC 10–11

Notices, Claims & Medical Examination

SEC 10 Notice and Claim Limitation

Time Limit

Claim must be made within 2 years of the accident or within 2 years from the date of death

SEC 10A Fatal Accident — Employer's Statement

  • Commissioner learns of employee death → sends registered notice to employer
  • Employer must reply within 30 days in prescribed form (how accident happened, liability accepted or not)
  • If liable accepted → deposit compensation within 30 days of receiving notice
  • If liability denied → must give clear reasons in writing
  • Commissioner then makes enquiry and informs dependants of their right to claim

SEC 10B Report of Fatal/Serious Injury

  • Accident causing death or serious bodily injury on employer's premises → report to Commissioner within 7 days
  • Serious bodily injury means:
    • Permanent disability or loss of use of limb
    • Permanent damage/loss of sight or hearing
    • Fracture of any limb
    • Unable to attend work for more than 20 days
  • Does NOT apply to factories covered under ESI Act, 1948

SEC 11 Medical Examination — Rules

SituationConsequence
Employer offers free medical exam within 3 daysEmployee must attend
Employee refuses / obstructs examinationRight to compensation suspended during refusal period (unless valid reason)
Employee leaves workplace before examinationCompensation suspended until he returns and offers himself for examination
Employee under suspension dies without examinationCommissioner may still allow compensation to dependants
Employee refuses free treatment / disobeys doctor's ordersCompensation only for original injury level, not aggravated injury
SEC 12–14A

Contracting, Insolvency & Asset Protection

SEC 12 Contracting

  • Principal gives work to contractor for work ordinarily part of principal's business
  • Contractor's employee injured → principal is liable (same as if directly employed)
  • Compensation calculated on wages paid by actual employer (contractor)
  • Principal = employer for all legal purposes when claim is made against him

SEC 13 Remedies Against Stranger

  • If injury was caused by a third party's fault
  • The employer who paid compensation can recover the amount from the person truly responsible

SEC 14 Insolvency of Employer

  • Employer has insurance + becomes insolvent → workman gets rights against insurance company
  • Insurer must pay workman directly (but not beyond policy limit)
  • Compensation due to workman gets top priority among all debts in insolvency
  • Policy void due to employer's fault (not premium) → workman still gets compensation from insurer
Sec 14A — First Charge on Transferred Assets

If employer transfers property before paying owed compensation, the unpaid compensation becomes the first charge (top priority claim) on the immovable property transferred — must be paid before all other claims.

Sec 17 — Contracting Out is INVALID

Any contract in which a worker gives up his right to compensation is void and has no legal effect. An employer cannot force a worker to sign away his compensation rights.

SEC 15–15B

Special Provisions — Seamen, Aircraft & Abroad

Section Applies To Notice Rule Death Claim Deadline Special Rules
§ 15 Masters of ships & Seamen Seaman gives notice to master of ship (master = employer). If accident on ship → no notice needed 1 year from family receiving news. If ship lost with all crew → 18 months from date of loss No half-monthly payment if ship-owner already caring for seaman under merchant shipping law. No double benefits with War Pension schemes
§ 15A Captains & Crew of Aircraft Crew gives notice to captain (captain = employer). If accident on aircraft → no notice needed 1 year from family getting news. If aircraft lost with all crew → 18 months from date of loss Commissioner can accept late claims with good reason
§ 15B Workers abroad (Indian companies & motor vehicles) Notice to local agent of the Indian company or vehicle owner 1 year from family receiving news of death Covers drivers, helpers, cleaners, mechanics sent abroad with Indian motor vehicles
Sec 16 — Annual Returns

State Government may require employers to submit annual reports including: number of workers injured, compensation paid, and any other required details — in the prescribed format.

Failure to Maintain Records

For failing to: keep notice-book (§10(3)), send statement to Commissioner (§10A), send accident report (§10B), submit annual return (§16)

Advance to Dependants

Max 3 months' wages

CHAPTER III

Commissioners — Powers & Procedure

SEC 19 Reference to Commissioner

Disputes referred to Commissioner (Civil Courts have NO jurisdiction):

  • Who must pay compensation
  • Whether injured person is a workman or not
  • How much compensation should be paid
  • Duration of payment
  • Seriousness of injury/disability

SEC 20 Appointment of Commissioners

  • Appointed by State Government by notification in Official Gazette
  • Multiple Commissioners in same area → State Govt regulates distribution of business
  • Commissioner may seek experts with special knowledge to assist in inquiry
  • Every Commissioner is deemed a public servant (under IPC)

SEC 21 Venue of Proceedings

  • Proceedings handled by Commissioner of area where: accident happened, OR workman normally lives, OR employer's registered office is located
  • Case transfer (within/outside State) allowed — must send all documents and money to new Commissioner
  • If accident happened outside India → Commissioner of area where owner/agent or company's registered office is located

SEC 23 Commissioner's Powers

  • Has all powers of a Civil Court under Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

SEC 28 Registration of Agreements

  • Lump-sum agreement (esp. for women or legally disabled) → send to Commissioner
  • Commissioner registers after 7 days notice to both parties
  • Once registered → legally enforceable

SEC 30 Appeals to High Court

  • Appeal must involve an important legal question
  • Amount in dispute must be at least ₹300
  • Employer must first deposit full ordered amount + get certificate
  • Must be filed within 60 days
  • No appeal if both parties agreed to follow Commissioner's decision
Sec 24 — Who Can Appear Before Commissioner

A lawyer · Insurance company official · Registered trade union official · Factory Inspector or Mines Inspector · Any officer authorized by State Government — must have written authorization from the party.

CHAPTER IV

Rules & Publication

SEC 32 & 34 State Government Rules

  • State Government may make rules to carry out purposes of this Act
  • Draft must be published at least 3 months before finalization
  • Once finalized and published in Official Gazette → have force of law
  • Rules must be laid before State Legislature

SEC 36 Central Government Rules

  • Must be placed before both Houses of Parliament for 30 days
  • Both Houses may change or cancel the rule
  • Actions done before change/cancellation remain valid

How to Use Schedule IV — Relevant Factor
  • Find the employee's age on his last birthday before the date compensation became due
  • Locate that age in Column 1 of Schedule IV
  • The corresponding number in Column 2 is the Relevant Factor
  • Multiply: 50% (death) or 60% (PTD) × Monthly Wages × Relevant Factor
  • Compare with minimum floor (₹1.2L for death, ₹1.4L for PTD) → pay whichever is more
