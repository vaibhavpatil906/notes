Attendance & Holidays

  • Minimum compulsory attendance for final trade test eligibility: 80% of actual working days
  • Trainees allowed same holidays as fixed by State Director for observance by staff
  • Stipend holders get stipend at full rate during holidays
  • 7.5 hrs/day fixed for uniformity across all ITIs in India
  • Transfer of trainees sanctioned by State Director — no expense on travel, no change in sanctioned numbers
  • Tuition fee decided by State Government based on State Council for Vocational Training recommendation

National Trade Certificate (NTC)

  • Awarded by National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT) to trainees who pass the trade test
  • Certificates printed with signature of the Secretary of NCVT
  • Digitally signed and verifiable via NCVT MIS portal

Record Cards of Pass-Out Trainees

  • Record cards maintained for all ex-trainees
  • Must track whether trainee has secured employment
  • If employed → name of employer recorded; if unemployed → whereabouts shown
INSPECTION

Inspection of ITI / ITCs

Inspection Frequencies

  • State Directorate inspecting officers should visit institutes as frequently as possible
  • Conduct trade tests for trainees from time to time
  • Check efficiency of instructors; assist Principals in all training matters
  • Each training centre inspected at least once a quarter by State Directorate officer
  • At least one centre per year inspected by tripartite team

Factors for Location of New ITIs/ITCs

  • 1. Availability of plentiful supply of electric power
  • 2. Employment potential in the trade(s)
  • 3. Industrial concentration or potentiality for industry in the area
  • 4. Availability of suitable land
PRINCIPAL

Suggested Duties of ITI Principal

Administrative Duties

  • Ensure all instructions from higher authorities are properly and expeditiously carried out
  • Accounts maintained properly; stores accounted for and verified periodically
  • Purchases according to specification and in good condition
  • Raw materials purchased in time and duly supplied
  • Machine and equipment properly maintained
  • Manufactured products accounted for and disposed per rules
  • Ensure foremen/supervisors maintain close supervision over instructors
  • Proper discipline maintained; proper security and safety precautions observed

Training & Welfare Duties

  • Training programmes carried out according to schemes
  • Close relationship maintained between trainees and instructional staff
  • Proper follow-up maintained of passed-out trainees
  • Trainees get proper medical aid and welfare arrangements
  • Proper facilities made available to inspection staff of State Directorate, DGE&T and other authorised bodies

Reference Material in Principal's Office (9 items)

  • 1. Layout plan of the institute
  • 2. Workshop layout showing machine locations
  • 3. Layout of electrical installation in workshop
  • 4. Electrical distribution layout of institute premises
  • 5. Organisational charts of the institute
  • 6. Prescribed syllabi and standard tools/equipment lists
  • 7. Trade-wise chart of trainees who passed trade test
  • 8. Duty charts of instructional/supervisory staff
  • 9. Weekly working time-table of each section

Reference Material in Workshop Sections (9 items)

  • 1. Sectional layout plan (machine positions, specs, loads)
  • 2. Prescribed syllabus and tools/equipment list
  • 3. Month/week-wise syllabus break-up charts
  • 4. Weekly working time-table
  • 5. Section progress card (individual trainee exercises)
  • 6. Visual aids, charts, drawings and models
  • 7. Machine and equipment maintenance instructions
  • 8. Chart showing safety rules
  • 9. First aid instructions
Instructional Media Packages (IMPs)

Government of India prescribes IMPs prepared by Central Instructional Media Institute (CIMI), Madras for all trades — used in ITIs as well as industrial establishments implementing the Apprenticeship Training programme.

NCVT

Terms & Conditions for NCVT Affiliation

Conditions to Maintain Affiliation

  • Must adopt NCVT standards: syllabi, scale of tools/equipment, shop layouts, training methods, trade testing
  • Training sessions must conform to All-India dates as laid down for starting sessions
  • Trainees appear in All India Trade Test conducted by NCVT
  • Trade Tests conducted in institute premises or centre approved by State Director per NCVT procedure
  • Institute must provide facilities for regular inspections by State and Central Government officers
  • No new trade or unit without prior approval of State Director

Conditions That Can Cause Withdrawal of Affiliation

  • Untrained instructors must be trained within 3 years of appointment — failing which, affiliation liable to be withdrawn
  • Closing a trade: State Director must give 6 months advance notice to DGE&T with reasons
  • Failure to maintain prescribed NCVT standards → affiliation withdrawn on recommendation of Standing Committee + acceptance by NCVT
  • Cost of raw materials for trade tests in private institutes borne by private institutes
NEW ITI

Opening New Training Institutes

For Opening New Training Institutes

  • Should be located in industrial estates or close proximity to them
  • Capacity: not more than 1000 seats and not less than 200 seats

For Introduction of New Trades / Change of Trades

  • Utilise existing idle capacity by converting surplus seats from low-demand to high-demand trades
  • Measure present demand by number of applications received at time of admission
  • Consider employment potential or availability of further apprenticeship training facilities
All India Skills Competition

Started in 1964 to foster healthy competition among trainees and raise skill standards. Only trainees under the regular Craftsmen Training Scheme are eligible to compete.

Training in Dual Trades

An ex-trainee who already holds a National Trade Certificate (NTC) in one trade under CTS may be admitted to train in another trade, provided they fulfill all conditions regarding educational qualifications, age, etc. prescribed for the new trade.
