📖WHAT IS EDUCATION?
Etymology
Latin educare = "to bring up" or "rear"
Latin educere = "to bring out" or "lead forth"
Both meanings: education is about rearing AND bringing out the potentialities of an individual
Definition
Education is a deliberate and systematic attempt to socialize an individual. It is a process through which knowledge is acquired and passed on from one generation to another.
Education as a Tool for Social Change
Mahatma Gandhi — "education leads to all-round development." Introduced Basic Education — integrated schooling with productive/useful work. Goal: school education self-reliant + school as productive institution.
Amartya Sen (Nobel Laureate) — Education is a basic capability that enhances the freedom to lead a valued life. His Capability Approach catalysed holistic understanding of human development.
Three Types of Education
1. FORMAL
Knowledge in institutionalized settings (schools, colleges). Standardized curriculum, pre-decided, different levels. Access to next level requires prior completion.
2. INFORMAL
Family, relatives impart knowledge to children at home. No formal curriculum.
3. NON-FORMAL
For out-of-school children unable to access formal education. Instruction outside formal system. Ex. NGOs, adult literacy, vocational training, skill development.

Social Mobility
Social mobility = shift in an individual's social status from one status to another. Can be higher/lower, inter/intra-generational.
Types:
Horizontal — occupation changes, status unchanged. Ex. doctor → medical teacher
Vertical — change in occupational/political/religious status → change in societal position
Upward — lower → higher position | Reverse = Downward Mobility
Inter-generational — social position changes generation to generation. Ex. factory worker father → lawyer/doctor son
Intra-generational — change within lifespan of a single generation / between siblings
Global Social Mobility Index
Prepared by World Economic Forum
First released in 2020
5 Determinants: HealthEducationTechnologyWorkProtection & Institutions

🏛️EVOLUTION OF GENERAL EDUCATION (British Period)
1781
Governor-General Warren Hastings established Calcutta Madarasa for Islamic law studies — first educational institute by EIC governance
1784
Asiatic Society of Bengal founded by William Jones to study India's history and culture. Charles Wilkins translated Bhagavad Gita to English.
1791
Jonathan Duncan (resident of Benares) founded Sanskrit College for study of Hindu laws and philosophies
1800
Governor-General Richard Wellesley founded Fort William College, Calcutta — to train EIC civil servants in Indian languages & customs
1813
Charter Act of 1813 — Set aside annual sum of ₹1 lakh for educating Indian subjects
1835
Macaulay's Minutes — Spend resources on Western sciences/literature in English only. English as medium of education. Mass education neglected. Downward Filtration Theory: educate a small upper/middle-class section as bridge between masses and government.
1854
Wood's Dispatch = "Magna Carta of English Education in India". Sacred duty of government to provide education. Masses in mother tongue but English required. Grants-in-aid system. First universities at Kolkata, Chennai, Mumbai.
1868 & 1875
Lord Mayo — Rajkot College, Kathiawar (1868) and Mayo College, Ajmer (1875) for political training of Indian princes and elites
1882
Hunter's Commission — Recommended withdrawal of government from higher education (private enterprise to take over). Moral side of education stressed. Primary education control transferred to district/municipal boards. Secondary divided into Literary and Vocational streams.
1902
Raleigh Commission (by Viceroy Curzon) — universities seen as factories of revolutionary ideology. Led to Indian Universities Act 1904.
1904
Indian Universities Act — Focus on study/research. Fellows reduced, nominated by government. Government acquired veto over senate. Stricter affiliation rules.
1910
Gopal Krishna Gokhale mooted idea of free and compulsory primary education for all children
1917
Sadler Commission — Stressed need for substantial increase in output of trained teachers
1917–19
Saddler University Commission — Initially for Calcutta University; reviewed all universities. School = 12 years. University entry after intermediate (not matric) for 3-year degree. Separate board for secondary & intermediate. 7 new universities 1916–21: Mysore, Patna, Benares, Aligarh, Dacca, Lucknow, Osmania.
1920
CABE (Central Advisory Board of Education) set up to advise government on education issues
1937
Wardha Scheme of Basic Education — By INC, committee under Zakir Hussain. Learning through activity (Gandhiji's ideas, Harijan magazine 1933). Three basic resolutions: 1) 8-year Free & Compulsory Education, 2) Mother Tongue as medium, 3) Education centred on manual/productive work.
1937–39
CABE stressed need for adult education on wider scale, suggested voluntary organizations' involvement
1944
Sergeant Plan of Education (by CABE) — State's responsibility for adult education. Free primary 3–6 yrs; Compulsory 6–11 yrs; High school for 11–17 yrs. Focus on teachers' training, physical education, handicapped children.

Anglo-vernacular high schools
Affiliated college at district level
Universities in presidency towns
Gave impetus to female education and vocational training
Secular education in government schools
Grants-in-aid system for educational institutions

📝WARDHA SCHEME — KEY FEATURES (1937)
Free & Compulsory education for 8 years (age 6–14)
First 5 years = Junior Stage, next 3 years = Senior Stage
Irrespective of gender, culture, or religion (girls allowed to leave at 12 if parents desire)
Mother Tongue as main medium of instruction
Flexible curriculum, free environment for child
Education with regional craftwork / productive work (Work-centric)
Craftwork to inculcate collective activity, dignity of labour, emotional development

🎒EARLY CHILDHOOD CARE & EDUCATION (ECCE)
Integral component of pre-primary education
NPE 1986 — government to take care of early childhood education, provide grants, monitoring
Article 45 (Constitution Act 2002) — "State shall endeavour to provide ECCE for all children until age six years"
RTE Act — Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education came into force: 1st April 2010
RTE provides free pre-school education for children above 3 years until 6 years
Global Level
UNESCO — Global Partnership Strategy for Early Childhood 2021–2030
World Conference on ECCE — Tashkent, Uzbekistan, November 2022. Tashkent Declaration adopted. UNESCO members pledged ≥10% total education spending on pre-primary.

🌱MONTESSORI EDUCATION
Founded by Italian physician Maria Montessori (1870–1952) in early 20th century
Based on children's natural interests and activities (NOT formal teaching)
Emphasis on hands-on learning and real-world skills
Child-centered approach — each lesson revolves around student's unique interests
Components
Multi-Age Groupings of Children
Student Choice of Activity
Uninterrupted Blocks of Time to Engage in Activities
4 Core Principles
1. Respect for the Child 2. Sensitive Periods (Stages/Planes) for Learning 3. A Prepared Environment 4. Independence and Discovery
Advantages
Enthusiastic, self-motivated learners
Improved social and problem-solving skills
Advanced mathematical understanding and outstanding reading abilities
Compassion, high self-esteem, and good manners

🌐UNIVERSALIZATION OF EDUCATION (UEE)
UEE = Making education up to Class I–VIII available to all children aged 6–14
6–11 years = Universalization of Primary Education
6–14 years = Universalization of Elementary Education
Article 45 (DPSP) and Article 21A (Fundamental Right) — constitutional basis for UEE
NPE 1968, 1986, 1992 all reiterated government commitment to UEE
4 Components of UEE
1. Access 2. Enrolment 3. Retention 4. Attainment
MPSC Rajyaseva 2022
Under UEE, which is NOT a major component?
Universal accessUniversal retentionUniversal enrolmentUniversal supervision ✓ (NOT)
Universalisation of Retention
Once a child joins, s/he should remain until primary school completion
If child leaves mid-way, idea of universalisation stands defeated
Mere enrolment is not enough — regular attendance and completion required
⚠️CAUSES OF SCHOOL DROP-OUTS IN INDIA
Child Labour — forced to work in agriculture, manufacturing, domestic service
Child Marriages — especially girls. Note: India has highest number of girls married before 18 (34% — UNICEF 2023)
Lack of school relevance — uninteresting studies → dropout
Gender disparities — safety concerns, household responsibilities, societal norms
Health issues — malnutrition, chronic illness
Lack of parental involvement — lack of awareness or economic pressure
Strategies to Achieve UEE
Overcome school dropout — emphasize retention and achievement, not mere enrolment
Strengthen non-formal education for working children, girls, marginalized sections
Decentralization of education
Shift focus from educationally backward states to educationally backward districts

Universal Declaration of Human Rights
Approved by UN General Assembly on 10th Dec 1948
Completed 75 years in 2023
10th December = Human Rights Day
Article 26 (Universal Declaration of Human Rights)
Everyone has the right to education. Education shall be free at least in elementary and fundamental stages.
Technical and professional education generally available; higher education accessible to all on merit basis
Education directed to full development of human personality + strengthening human rights
Parents have prior right to choose the kind of education for their children
6 Dakar Goals
1. Expand and improve ECCE for most vulnerable children
2. By 2015: all children access free compulsory primary education of good quality
3. Learning needs of all young people/adults met through equitable access
4. 50% improvement in adult literacy by 2015, especially women
5. Eliminate gender disparities in primary/secondary by 2005; gender equality in education by 2015
6. Improve quality of education so measurable learning outcomes achieved by all
