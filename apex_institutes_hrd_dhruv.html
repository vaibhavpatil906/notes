01 · Vocational Training — Apex Body
Directorate General of Training DGT
Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE)
⭐ Apex Organisation — Vocational Training 📍 Under MSDE Bifurcated from DGE&T in 2015
Core Role
DGT is the apex organisation for development and coordination at National level for all programmes relating to vocational training. ITIs are under administrative and financial control of State Governments/UT Administrations. DGT also operates Vocational Training Schemes in specialised areas through field institutes under its direct control. Common policies, common standards and procedures, training of instructors and trade testing are the responsibility of DGT.
Development Timeline
July 1945
Directorate General of Resettlement and Employment (DGRE) was set up. Purpose: Post–World War II resettlement. Note: Training was NOT its initial purpose.
1948
Employment Exchanges under DGRE were opened to all categories of job-seekers.
1950
Training services for civilians were added. Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) started. Training provided through Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs).
1956
Administrative control of Employment Exchanges and ITIs was given to State/UT Governments on recommendation of Shiva Rao Committee (1952).
1960
DGRE renamed as DGE&T (Directorate General of Employment & Training).
1969
Full financial responsibility of Employment Exchanges and ITIs handed over to States/UTs.
2015
DGE&T was bifurcated into: DGT (Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship) + DGE (Ministry of Labour and Employment).
Functions of DGT
Frame overall policies, norms and standards for vocational training
Diversify, update and expand training facilities — craftsmen and crafts instructor training
Organise and conduct specialised training and research at specially established training institutes
Implement, regulate and increase scope of training of apprentices under Apprentices Act, 1961
Organise vocational training programmes for women
Provide vocational guidance and employment counselling
Assist SC/ST and persons with disabilities — enhance capabilities for wage employment and self-employment

02 · National Regulator — VET
National Council for Vocational Education and Training NCVET
Established as a Regulatory Body by Government of India
📅 Established: 5 December 2018 ✅ Fully Operational: 1 August 2020 🏛️ Statutory Regulatory Body
What is NCVET?
Overarching national regulator with the aim of setting standards, developing comprehensive regulations, and improving the vocational education, training, and skilling ecosystem. Primary objective: ensure strong industry interfacing and implement effective regulations that enhance quality and outcomes of VET. It is responsible for development, qualitative improvement, and regulation of vocational education and training systems. It also grants recognition to and monitors the functioning of awarding bodies, assessment agencies, and skill information providers.
Mandates of NCVET
  • Recognising, disciplining, and de-recognising Awarding Bodies (ABs), Assessment Agencies (AAs), and Skill-related Information Providers (SIPs)
  • Establishing regulations and guidelines for Skill Universities as ABs and AAs in consultation with MSDE
  • Anchoring the NSQF (National Skills Qualifications Framework) — a framework that organises qualifications based on levels of knowledge, skills, and competencies
  • Maintaining the National Qualification Register (NQR) — acts as a repository for all qualifications aligned to the NSQF
  • Approving NSQF-aligned qualifications and National Occupational Standards (NOSs) developed by ABs, including Sector Skill Councils (SSCs)
  • Performs other incidental functions as specified in its resolution

03 · National Campaign
Skill India Mission
Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE)
📅 Approved by Cabinet: 01 July 2015 🚀 Launched: 15 July 2015 — World Youth Skills Day Implemented through MSDE
3-Tier Institutional Structure
Tier 1: Governing Council — policy guidance at apex level
Tier 2: Steering Committee
Tier 3: Mission Directorate + Executive Committee (executive arm)

Mission Directorate supported by three institutions: NSDA + NSDC + DGT
National Policy on Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, 2015 — Objectives
  • Core objective: Empower the individual — enable realisation of full potential through lifelong learning
  • Make quality vocational training aspirational for both youth and employers — youth sees it as matter of choice; employer acknowledges productivity and pays premium
  • Ensure both vertical and horizontal pathways for skilled workforce — seamless integration of skill training with formal education
  • Focus on outcome-based approach — increased employability, better livelihoods, improved productivity across primary, secondary and tertiary sectors
  • Increase capacity and quality of training infrastructure and trainers — equitable and easy access to every citizen
  • Address human resource needs by aligning supply of skilled workers with sectoral requirements — including flagship programmes like Make in India
  • Establish IT-based Labour Market Information System (LMIS) — for aggregating demand and supply of skilled workforce, matching supply with demand
  • Recognise value of on-the-job training — make apprenticeships in actual work environments an integral part of all skill development efforts
  • Ensure skilling needs of socially and geographically disadvantaged and marginalised groups — SCs, STs, OBCs, minorities, differently abled persons
  • National Skills Universities and institutes promoted in partnership with States as centres of excellence for skill development and training of trainers
  • ITI qualifications linked to formal educational qualifications through language/bridge courses in consultation with State/Central boards
  • Existing ITIs and Polytechnics to be modernised with curriculum aligned to emerging competency-based market demand; technology leveraged for pedagogical techniques
  • ITIs interface with industry promoted to enhance apprenticeship opportunities, improve relevance and increase employability
  • Industry expected to lead ITIs through Institute Management Committees (IMCs) — given autonomy to implement decisions
  • New ITIs set up in PPP mode in unserved blocks; higher order skilling through ATIs and Multi Skill Institutes (MSIs) in PPP mode — near demand centres as Skill Hubs (affiliated to Skill Universities)
  • State Governments encouraged to set up Kaushal Vardhan Kendras (KVKs) at panchayat level — linked to nearest ITI/MSI/ATI for capacity building, curriculum development, assessment and certification; also function as counselling/guidance centres
Parameters to Improve Quality of Skilling
Quality Assurance Framework embedded in NSQF Market Relevant Training Programmes Recognition of Prior Learning Curriculum Alignment National Certification Framework Employability Skills Placements
Labour Market Information System (LMIS)
  • Integrated database with socio-economic data modules: (i) supply side skilled labour force statistics, (ii) demand of skilled/unskilled labour, (iii) market trends — wage structures, economic growth, occupational shortages
  • Integrates all existing databases: NSDC SDMS, National Career Service Portal, NCVT MIS portal, SDIS portal, State databases, SSC databases
  • A National Skills Research Division (NSRD) constituted within NSDA — conducts skill surveys, aggregates SSC environmental scans, studies emerging demand trends, operates LMIS
  • NSRD involves private sector subject experts — serves as strategy think tank for MSDE
National Entrepreneurship Hub (E-Hub)
  • Advised by a National Advisory Committee (NAC) comprising representatives from government ministries, entrepreneurs, NGOs and academia
  • Purpose: To support entrepreneurs in India
Key Initiatives Under Skill India
  • PMKVY — Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana: short-term skill training for youth
  • Common Norms — uniformity and standardisation in implementation of skill development schemes across Central Ministries/Departments
  • Sector Skill Councils (SSCs) — industry-led bodies to develop National Occupation Standards (NOS)
  • SANKALP — Skills Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Promotion: outcome-oriented World Bank supported project for strengthening institutional mechanisms at national and state levels
  • STRIVE — Skills Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement: World Bank assisted project to improve relevance and efficiency of skills training through ITIs and apprenticeships
  • Skill India Portal — provides information of trainees, training providers and trainers
  • Kaushal Rozgar Melas / Skills Career Counselling Scheme — outreach activities to make skilling aspirational

What is a Skill University?
Type of Higher-Education degree-awarding institute offering courses that are skill-based, job-oriented and of applied nature. Offer courses with higher job potential and employability. Degrees conform to the National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF). The idea was first proposed in the National Skills Universities Bill, 2015 by MSDE, Government of India.

05 · Autonomous Body — NSQF Anchor
National Skill Development Agency NSDA
Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship | Registered under Societies Registration Act, 1860
📅 Cabinet Approval: 9 May 2013 Autonomous Body — Societies Act 1860 Anchors NSQF + NSQC Secretariat
Core Role
Anchors the National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF) and allied quality assurance mechanisms for synergising skill initiatives. Nodal agency for State Skill Development Missions. NSQF is implemented through the National Skill Qualification Committee (NSQC); permanent secretariat for NSQC is set up in NSDA.
Functions of NSDA
  • Take all possible steps to meet skilling targets as envisaged in the 12th Five Year Plan and beyond
  • Coordinate and harmonise the approach to skill development among Central Ministries/Departments, State Governments, NSDC and private sector
  • Anchor and operationalise the NSQF — ensure quality and standards meet sector specific requirements
  • Be the nodal agency for State Skill Development Missions
  • Raise extra-budgetary resources for skill development from international agencies, multilateral agencies and private sector
  • Evaluate existing skill development schemes — assess efficacy and suggest corrective action
  • Create and maintain a national database related to skill development including development of a dynamic Labour Market Information System (LMIS)
  • Take affirmative action for advocacy
  • Ensure skilling needs of disadvantaged and marginalised groups — SCs, STs, OBCs, minorities, women, differently abled
  • Discharge any other function assigned by Government of India
  • The NSQF is anchored in NSDA and implemented through the National Skill Qualification Committee (NSQC); permanent secretariat for NSQC set up in NSDA

06 · Public Private Partnership
National Skill Development Corporation NSDC
Not-for-Profit Public Limited Company | HQ: New Delhi
📅 Incorporated: 31 July 2008 Under Section 25, Companies Act 1956 Set up by Ministry of Finance
Shareholding — PPP Model
Government of India (through MSDE): 49%  |  Private Sector: 51%
Aim: Promote skill development by catalysing creation of large, quality and for-profit vocational institutions.
Key Initiatives by NSDC
  • Kaushal Mart — online skilling resource marketplace; aggregates skilling resources and bridges demand-supply gap
  • Kaushal e-Pustakalaya (eBook Reader) — Android App giving readers and skill seekers access to skilling content on the move
  • Takshashila Portal — dedicated online platform for management of Trainers and Assessors of Indian Short-Term Skill ecosystem; central repository for quality Trainers and Assessors
  • Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendra (PMKK) — MSDE established Model Training Centres (MTCs) in every district; NSDC is implementing agency. Objectives: (i) establish distinguished institutions showcasing aspirational value of competency-based skill training, (ii) emphasise quality, sustainability and stakeholder engagement, (iii) shift from mandate-driven temporary model to sustainable institutional framework
  • Rozgar Mela — event where employers and job seekers come together for applying and interviewing. Employment strategy to fast-track meeting of job seekers and employers. Range: 5–100 employers with hundreds of job seekers. NSDC conducts at national level
  • Skill India Portal (SIP) — formerly SMART (Skill Management Accreditation of Training Centres). Any entity can create a training centre and apply for affiliation/accreditation after registering as Training Provider. Final decision on accreditation rests with aligned Sector Skill Council. Onboarding done by NSDC
  • Technical Intern Training Program (TITP) — initiated in Japan in 1993; supports developing countries by hosting citizens and imparting skills through On-the-Job Training (OJT). MSDE signed MoC with Japan's Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare on 17 October 2017. Indian individuals selected for 3–5 year internships in Japan. Upon completion must return and apply acquired skills in India. NSDC appointed monitoring body in January 2018
  • Udaan Scheme — Special Industry Initiative (SII) for J&K; funded by Ministry of Home Affairs; implemented by NSDC. Based on Rangarajan Committee recommendations. Makes educated youth (graduates, post-graduates, 3-year engineering diploma holders) of J&K employable. Launched FY 2011-12

  • Act as nodal agency for achieving goals of Universalisation of Elementary Education

TITI is a category of institutes, not a single autonomous apex body. Established under DGE&T (now DGT), Government of India. ATI Mumbai is treated as the TITI-equivalent institute in Maharashtra.
  • Institutes meant for training and upgrading ITI instructors and technical teachers

15 · Technical Education Regulator
All India Council for Technical Education AICTE
Statutory Body | Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education | HQ: New Delhi
📅 Est. November 1945 — Advisory Body Statutory Status: Act of Parliament 1987 📍 HQ: New Delhi Dept of Higher Education, MoE
Core Mandate
Set up in November 1945 as a national-level apex advisory body to conduct a survey on facilities available for technical education and to promote development in a coordinated and integrated manner. Responsible for proper planning and coordinated development of technical education and management education system in India.
Objectives of AICTE
  • Create, implement and maintain the norms and standards by acting as a statutory authority
  • Offer quality assurance through accreditation in institutes
  • Understand the need and provide funding for priority areas
  • Maintain parity of certification and awards in institutes
  • Implement and develop the technical education system of India

Functions of AICTE
  • Undertake survey in various fields of technical education; collect data on all related matters
  • Coordinate the development of technical education in the country at all levels
  • Allocate and disburse grants from the Fund of the Council to technical institutions and Universities imparting technical education
  • Formulate schemes for promoting technical education for women, handicapped and weaker sections
  • Evolve suitable performance appraisal systems for technical institutions and Universities; norms for accountability
  • Lay down norms and standards for courses, curricula, physical and instructional facilities, staff pattern, qualifications, quality instructions, assessment and examinations
  • Fix norms and guidelines for charging tuition and other fees
  • Grant approval for starting new technical institutions and introduction of new courses/programmes
  • Lay down norms for granting autonomy to technical institutions
  • Take all necessary steps to prevent commercialisation of technical education

National Board of Accreditation (NBA)
  • One of the two major bodies responsible for accreditation of higher education institutions in India — along with NAAC

  • Established by AICTE in 1994; HQ: New Delhi
  • NBA accredits programmes, NOT institutes
  • Accredited programmes include: diplomas, UG and PG programmes
  • Accredited fields: Engineering & Technology, Management, Pharmacy, Architecture, Applied Arts and Crafts, Computer Applications, Hospitality and Tourism Management
National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC)
  • Government organisation that assesses and accredits Higher Education Institutions (HEIs)
  • Autonomous body funded by UGC
  • Established in 1994; HQ: Bangalore

18 · Industry-Led Bodies
Sector Skill Councils SSC
Autonomous Bodies (Section 8 Company or Society) | NSDC provides Seed Funding
Autonomous Body — Section 8 Company or Society NSDC: Initial Seed Funding + Incubation Bridge Industry Demand ↔ Skilling Requirements
Core Role
SSCs play a vital role in bridging the gap between what industry demands and what skilling requirements should be. Operate as autonomous bodies. NSDC is mandated to initiate and incubate SSCs with initial seed funding to facilitate their growth and enable them to achieve self-sustainability in a time-bound manner.
Mandate / Functions of SSC
  • Identification of skill development needs — prepare catalogue of types of skills, range and depth of skills to facilitate individual choice
  • Development of a sector skill development plan and maintaining skill inventory
  • Determine skills/competency standards and qualifications and get them notified as per NSQF
  • Standardisation of affiliation, accreditation, examination and certification process in accordance with NSQF as determined by NSQC
  • May also conduct skill-based assessment and certification for QP/NOS aligned training programmes
  • Participation in the setting up of affiliation, accreditation, examination and certification norms for their respective sectors
  • Plan and facilitate execution of Training of Trainers along with NSDC and states
  • Promotion of academies of excellence
  • Paying particular attention to the skilling needs of ST/SC, differently-abled and minority groups
  • Ensuring that persons trained and skilled as per norms are assured of employment at decent wages
Examples of Sector Skill Councils
Aerospace and Aviation SSC Agriculture Skill Council of India Automotive Skill Development Council Beauty and Wellness SSC Electronics SSC of India

📅 First: Kolkata (1965) 4 Institutes: Kolkata, Chandigarh, Bhopal, Chennai National Status: 2003 Motto: Providing Excellence in Technical Education
Origin
Established in the country as Technical Teachers' Training Institute (TTTI). First established in Kolkata (1965) (others at Chandigarh, Bhopal & Chennai). Established by Dept of Education, MHRD, Govt of India as fully centrally funded autonomous institutions. In 2003, Govt of India accorded national status in recognition of contribution for overall quality improvement of Technical Education System.
Mandate
  • Providing pre & in-service training to teachers and staff of Degree and Diploma level training institutions
  • Conducting activities related to quality improvement of the technical education system of the country
5 Focal Activities
Education & Short-term Training
Curriculum Development
Learning Resources Development
Research & Development
Extension Services & Consultancy
Objectives of NITTTR
  • Provide professional education and training for teachers of engineering and technology disciplines in technical institutions
  • Strive for continuous improvement in instruction and research in engineering and technology disciplines and research in management of technical education
  • Actively support growth and quality improvement of technical education through involvement at national and state levels
  • Act as a centre for offering quality training programs — covering the entire gamut: polytechnics, engineering colleges, vocational and management education at national level

23 · School Boards & Education Bodies
CBSE, KVS, NCTE, JNV, NIOS, Maharashtra SIVE CBSE
Under Department of School Education and Literacy, Ministry of Education
CBSE — Central Board of Secondary Education
  • National level board of education for public and private schools, controlled and managed by Government of India
  • Established in 1929 by a Government Resolution
  • HQ: New Delhi
  • CBSE affiliates all Kendriya Vidyalayas, all Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas, private schools, and most schools approved by the Central Government
KVS — Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan
  • System of central government schools under the Ministry of Education
  • Established in 1963
  • As of April 2023: 1,253 schools in India + 3 abroad (Kathmandu, Moscow and Tehran)
  • Non-profit organisation
  • All schools affiliated to CBSE
  • Original objective: educate children of Indian Defence Services personnel posted to remote locations; later extended to all central government employees
  • HQ: New Delhi
  • Chairman: Minister in Charge of Ministry of Education | Deputy Chairman: Minister of State, Ministry of Education
  • Salient Features: Common textbooks and bilingual medium of instruction; All KVs co-educational and composite; Sanskrit taught in Class VI–VIII; No tuition fee for boys (up to Class VIII), girls (up to Class XII), SC/ST students and children of KVS employees
NCTE — National Council for Teacher Education
  • Statutory body set up under the National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993
  • Established in 1995
  • HQ: New Delhi | Under Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education
  • Mandate: achieve planned and coordinated development of teacher education throughout the country; govern regulation and maintenance of norms and standards for teacher education
  • Functions: Undertake surveys and studies on teacher education; Make recommendations to Central/State Governments and UGC; Co-ordinate and monitor teacher education development; Lay down guidelines for minimum qualifications for teachers; Lay down norms for teacher education courses (eligibility criteria, duration, course contents, mode of curriculum); Evolve performance appraisal system for recognised institutions; Take steps to prevent commercialisation of teacher education
NEP 2020 — Teacher-Related Provisions
  • Promulgates that teacher is at the centre of all fundamental reforms in education
  • Recommends 50 hours of Continuous Professional Development (CPD) for teachers, Principals and teacher-educators
  • Aims at making optimum use of latest pedagogy; provides autonomy to choose offline or online activities/trainings as per need
National Foundation for Teachers' Welfare
  • Charitable foundation established by the Government of India
  • Established in 1962
  • Responsible for providing "relief to teachers and their dependants who may be in indigent circumstances"
  • HQ: Hyderabad
JNV — Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya
  • System of central schools for talented students predominantly from rural areas in India
  • Run by Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti — autonomous organisation under the Department of School Education and Literacy, Ministry of Education
  • Established in 1986
  • Fully residential and co-educational schools affiliated to CBSE — Classes VI to XII
  • Chairman of the Samiti = Minister of Education
DJSS — Directorate of Jan Shikshan Sansthan
  • Sub-ordinate office of MSDE — responsible for monitoring and supervision of Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme
  • JSS formerly known as Shramik Vidyapeeth
  • Implemented since 1967; renamed Jan Shikshan Sansthan in 2000
  • JSS transferred from Ministry of Education to MSDE in July 2018
  • Implemented through NGOs with 100% grants from Government of India
  • Mandate: provide vocational skills in non-formal mode to non-literate, neo-literates, persons with education up to 8th, school drop-outs up to 12th in age group 15–45 years
  • Priority groups: Women, SC, ST, minorities, other backward sections
  • Registered under Societies Registration Act, 1860
  • DJSS Roles: Approval of Annual Action Plan; Release of Grants; Technical support; Supervision & monitoring; Periodic visits; Guidance to Board of Management; Curriculum Development
Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training (SIVE Equivalent)
  • Government of Maharashtra established Maharashtra State Technical Education Examination Board (MSBTEE) in 1956 under Directorate of Technical Education — this is the SIVE-like institution in Maharashtra
  • Due to growth, Government of Maharashtra bifurcated DTE and established Directorate of Vocational Education and Training in 1986
  • MSBTEE also bifurcated — Maharashtra State Vocational Education Examination Board (MSBVEE) established in 1986
  • MSBVEE renamed and established as Maharashtra State Board of Skill, Vocational Education and Training from 2022 — to regulate matters pertaining to Skill, Vocational Education and Training and Entrepreneurship Education as per NSQF
  • Functions: Design and Approval of Course; Affiliation of Vocational Training Institutes; Assessment and Certification; Training Management, Research and Development
Viksit Bharat Shiksha Adhikshan Bill — Higher Education Reform
  • Union Cabinet approved this bill proposing a unified regulator for higher education — replacing existing statutory bodies UGC, AICTE, and NCTE
  • Earlier known as the Higher Education Commission of India (HECI) Bill
  • Follows recommendations of National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 for a single regulatory authority to streamline governance of higher education
  • Medical (National Medical Commission) and Legal education (Bar Council of India) continue to be regulated by their respective councils — remaining outside HECI's regulatory purview
