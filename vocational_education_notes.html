1. What is Vocational Education?

  • Education based on occupation and employment
  • Also known as Career and Technical Education (CTE) or Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET)
  • Prepares people for specific trades, crafts and careers at all levels
  • Also called Technical Education — trainee directly develops expertise in a group of techniques

Need of Vocational Education

  • Bridges skill gap — between industry demands and workforce skills
  • Enhances chances of gainful employment or self-employment
  • Increases industry productivity by aligning training with needs
  • Caters to hands-on / practical learners — broader spectrum of talent
  • Helps alleviate poverty by improving standard of living

3. ITI Ecosystem

📌 Directorate General of Training (DGT)

  • National-level apex organization for development & coordination of vocational training
  • Frames norms, policies and standards for vocational training programmes
  • Operates the Apprenticeship Act, 1961
  • Transferred in 2015 from Ministry of Labour & Employment (MoLE) to MSDE

ITI Accreditation Timeline

Pre-2012
Done by DGE&T as per NCVT norms. NCVT can reject applications.
2012
Accreditation by Quality Council of India (QCI)
July 2017
Responsibility shifted back from QCI → DGT
Post-2017
Accreditation by DGT, once every 5 years

📌 National Skill Training Institutes (NSTIs)

  • Craft Instructors Training Scheme (CITS) — initiated to strengthen instructor quality
  • First Craft Instructors' Training Institute: 1948
  • NSTIs started by DGE&T in 1963
  • In 2010 — States/UTs & private industries allowed to set up instructor training institutes
  • Also called Institute for Training of Trainers (ITOT)
  • Total: 18 institutes (6 government + 12 non-government)

📌 CTS vs CITS

Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) — Started 1950
Provides vocational training to develop skilled workers in various trades.
Target: Individuals seeking vocational skills.
Crafts Instructor Training Scheme (CITS) — Started 1948
Trains individuals to become skilled instructors/trainers/faculty for VET programs.
Target: Those who have already completed vocational training.

Maharashtra: 411 ITIs total | Over 2.6 lakh applications for 1.5 lakh seats.

📌 Institute Management Committee (IMC)

  • Converted by State Govt into a Society under Societies Registration Act
  • IMC registered under Society Registration Act 1860
  • IMCs given Financial & Academic Autonomy
  • IMC determines 20% of admissions
  • Central Govt releases interest-free loan of ₹2.5 crore directly to IMC

IMC Members

  • Industry Partner (IP) — Chairperson
  • 4 members from local industry nominated by IP
  • 5 members nominated by State Govt
  • Principal of ITI — Ex-officio Member Secretary
Guiding Principle: Decentralization and accountability of users | MoA signed among IP, State & Central Govt | IDP prepared by IMC with KPIs for 5 years
Maharashtra's 5 New ITIs: One each in Mumbai, Sambhaji Nagar, Pune, Nagpur, Nashik. Foreign languages taught for non-English speaking countries.

4. History of Vocational Education

India had a strong Guru-Shishya tradition of education and training. Millions still learn occupational skills this way.
1854

Wood's Despatch — First to highlight need for VE at secondary school stage

1882

Indian Education Commission (Hunter Commission) — Examined problems of VE & technical education

1929 & 1934

Hartog Review Committee (1929) & Sapru Enquiry Committee (1934) — Emphasized vital role of VE in economic development

1936

Wood-Abbot Advisory Committee — Basis for diploma-level technical education & establishment of polytechnics

1944

Sargent Report — Two streams: Academic & Technical

1948

Radhakrishnan Commission — VE to meet variety of needs of young men & women

1952–53

Mudaliar Commission — Multipurpose school; 11-year pattern; diversification after 8 years in crafts/vocations

1964–66

Kothari Commission — Proposed 10+2+3 pattern; recommended 50% students diverted to VE at +2 level within 20 years

1977

Working Group on Vocationalization — Headed by P. Sabanayagam; recommended National Council of VE&T

1985

Kulandaiswamy Working Group — Concept of vocationalization at different levels

1986

National Policy on Education (NPE 1986) — High priority to VE. Targets: 10% by 1990, 25% by 1995

1988

MCVC introduced by MHRD (idea from NPE 1986)

2005

NCF 2005 — Flexible vocational courses integrated with mainstream schooling

2008

NSDC established — promote private sector in skill development

2013

NVEQF subsumed into NSQF

2014

Creation of Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE)

2015

PMKVY 1.0 launched (15 July — World Youth Skills Day)

2020

NEP 2020 — Mainstreaming VE from Grade 6; target: 50% learners with VE exposure by 2025

📌 MCVC — Minimum Competency Vocational Courses

  • Job-oriented course after Class 10, duration: 2 years
  • 5 courses: Commerce Technology | Engineering Technology | Agriculture Technology | Health & Paramedical Technology | Home Science Technology
  • Introduced in India in 1988 by MHRD (idea: NPE 1986)
  • Renamed to HSC Vocational on 2nd March 2009
  • In 2018 — Maharashtra merged MCVC with ITIs

5. Pre-Vocational Education & SUPW

📌 Pre-Vocational Education

Basic exposure, awareness and training in simple work-related skills before formal VE. Not full-fledged VT — a preparatory phase.

Policy Scope (Class Range Over Time)

  • Kothari Commission (1964-66) & NPE 1968 — Classes IX–X
  • NPE 1986 & POA 1992 — Class VI to X
  • Samagra Shiksha & NEP 2020 — Classes VI–VIII (foundational) up to Class X
  • CBSE Pre-vocational — offered to Class 6, 7, 8 students

📌 Socially Useful Productive Work (SUPW)

Defined as purposive, meaningful, manual work resulting in goods or services useful to society.
Term recommended by: Ishwarbhai Patel Committee (1977)
Work experience concept originally from: Kothari Commission (1964-66)

Characteristics of SUPW

FeatureMeaning
PurposiveLearner understands "Why" and "for what" — performs intelligently
MeaningfulRelated to needs of learner and community
Manual WorkInstills dignity of labour
Goods or ServicesResults in material product OR service (remunerative or social)

SUPW Activities by Stage

Lower Primary
Paper folding/crafts | Campus cleaning | Community visits | Growing plants
Upper Primary
Stitching & knitting | School beautification | Making dolls | Clay models
Secondary
Repair of appliances | Garment making | Squash/Jam/Pickles | Cloth dyeing

6. Problems in Vocational Education

  • Shortage of VET Institutions — insufficient post-Independence
  • Limited Curriculum — only technical, no liberal education
  • Medium of Instruction — English dominates; difficult for Hindi/regional medium students
  • Lack of Quality Teachers — staff retention problem
  • Insufficient Practical Exposure — theoretical knowledge not enough
  • Shortage of Continuous Education — workforce becomes outdated
  • Lack of Coordination among Govt agencies & regulatory bodies
  • Inadequate Industry Linkage — poor communication between training sector and job providers

7. PMKVY — All Phases

PMKVY 1.0 · 2015

Launch Phase

  • Launched 15 July 2015 (World Youth Skills Day)
  • India's largest Skill Certification Scheme
  • Free short-duration training + monetary rewards
  • 19.86 lakh candidates trained
  • Components: STT, Special Projects, RPL
PMKVY 2.0 · 2016–2020

Expansion Phase

  • Target: 10 million youth
  • CSCM: 75% funds (by NSDC)
  • CSSM: 25% funds (by SSDMs)
  • 1.10 crore candidates trained
  • Standardise certification process
PMKVY 3.0 · 2021

Decentralised Phase

  • Decentralised structure
  • District Skill Committees (DSCs) key role
  • Demand-based approach
  • Trainee & learner centric
  • Target: 15–45 years | 7.37 lakh trained
PMKVY 4.0 · 2022–2026

Future Skills Phase

  • Part of Skill India Programme (₹8,800 crore)
  • Skill Hubs in CBSE institutions
  • Focus: AI, ML, new-age skills
  • Digital platforms for enrollment
  • Age: 15–45 years

📌 Key Components

Short Term Training (STT)
School/college dropouts or unemployed. 200–600 hrs. Placement assistance by Training Partners.
Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)
Age 18–59. 12–80 hrs. Aligns unregulated workforce to NSQF. 5-stage process. Certify with ≥70% marks.
Special Projects
Marginalized/vulnerable groups. 15–45 years. Training in special areas or non-standard job roles.
Skill India Programme (Feb 2025 Cabinet): PMKVY 4.0 + PM-NAPS + JSS Scheme combined under single scheme with ₹8,800 crore outlay (2022-23 to 2025-26)

8. Other Key Schemes & Institutions

📌 SDIS / MES Scheme

  • Also known as Modular Employable Skills (MES)
  • Improves employability via vocational training
  • Public-private partnership for school dropouts & unorganized sector workers
  • Training duration: 50 to 600 hours
  • Multi-entry and multi-exit allowed
  • Certification jointly by NCVT and Industry
  • Educational qualification: Class 5 to 12 pass
SDIS Efficiency assessed by: Employment rate | Placement records | Employer feedback | Pre/post skill assessment | Tracer studies

📌 Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS)

  • Formerly: Shramik Vidyapeeth
  • Implemented since 1967 | Renamed JSS in 2000
  • Transferred from Ministry of Education to MSDE in July 2018
  • Implemented through NGOs with 100% GoI grants
  • Target: Non-literate to Class 8 dropouts | Age: 15–45 years
  • Priority groups: Women, SC, ST, Minorities, OBC
  • Registered under Societies Registration Act, 1860
  • Supervised by DJSS (subordinate office of MSDE)

📌 SANKALP

  • Launched: 19th January 2018
  • Duration: 6 years till March 2023
  • Funded by World Bank
  • 4 Key Result Areas: Institutional Strengthening | Quality Assurance | Inclusion of marginalized | Expanding through PPPs

📌 PM YUVA Yojana

  • Full form: Yuva Udyamita Vikas Abhiyan
  • Centrally sponsored — entrepreneurship education
  • 5 years: 2016-17 to 2020-21
  • 7 lakh+ students through 3050 institutes
  • Uses MOOCs & LMS for free delivery
  • 3050 institutions: 2200 Higher Learning | 300 Schools | 500 ITIs | 50 EDCs

📌 Skill Loan Scheme (July 2015)

  • Institutional credit for NSQF-aligned skill courses
  • Applicable to all IBA member banks
  • Quantum: ₹5,000 to ₹1,50,000 | No collateral required
  • Repayment: ≤₹50k → 3 yrs | ₹50k–1L → 5 yrs | >₹1L → 7 yrs

📌 IGNOU & Others

  • IGNOU — Public research university, New Delhi | Est. 1985 | Motto: The People's University
  • IIE — Indian Institute of Entrepreneurship | Est. 1993, Guwahati | Transferred to MSDE on 2 May 2015
  • Skills Build Platform — Digital platform by IBM | India: 4th country (2019) | bharatskills.gov.in

9. Maharashtra-Specific

📌 Maharashtra State Skill Development Society (MSSDS)

  • Registered: 15th February 2011 under Societies Registration Act 1860
  • Single Nodal Agency for planning, coordination, execution & monitoring
  • Functions under Skill, Employment, Entrepreneurship and Innovation Department, GoM
  • All state skill development schemes integrated under single umbrella
  • Appoint "Maharashtra Skill Mission Ambassador"

📌 Women Entrepreneurship Cell (WEC)

  • Set up by Maharashtra State Innovation Society (MSInS) in January 2021
  • Focus: women entrepreneurship in Maharashtra
  • Dedicated women incubator in partnership with SNDT Women's University, Mumbai
  • Under Maharashtra State Innovative Startup Policy 2018 — grant of ₹5 Cr per incubator
