2. Quantitative & Qualitative Aspects

📌 Key Formulas & Concepts

Natural Growth = Births − Deaths
Actual Growth = Births − Deaths + In-Migration − Out-Migration
  • Growth of Population = Change in population between 2 time points
  • Growth Rate = Change expressed in percentage
  • Positive Growth: Birth Rate > Death Rate OR permanent in-migration
  • Negative Growth: Birth Rate < Death Rate OR out-migration
  • CBR = Crude Birth Rate = Live births per 1000 population per year
  • CDR = Crude Death Rate = Deaths per 1000 population per year

📌 4 Phases of Population Growth in India

Phase I · 1901–1921
Stagnant / Stationary Phase — Negative growth in 1911–21; both birth & death rates HIGH
Phase II · 1921–1951
Steady Growth — ↓ mortality (better health/sanitation); birth rate still HIGH
Phase III · 1951–1981
Population EXPLOSION — Rapid ↓ mortality + HIGH fertility; post-Independence development
Phase IV · Post 1981
Slow Growth — ↓ crude birth rate; higher marriage age; better female education
UN Projection: India peaks at ~1.7 billion in early 2060s, then declines by 12%

📌 Qualitative Aspects & Projections

  • Qualitative factors: Sex Ratio | Literacy | Standard of Living | Life Expectancy | Occupational Structure
  • 'Women & Men in India 2023' report published by MoSPI
  • India population projected → 152.2 Crore by 2036
  • Sex ratio projected → 952 women/1000 men by 2036
  • Population aged 60+ will substantially increase by 2036
  • Population under 15 yrs projected to DECREASE (declining fertility)

3. National Population Policy 2000

📌 Origin & Significance

Philosophy derived from ICPD held at Cairo, Egypt (1994)
India's first ever comprehensive & holistic population policy
TFR = Total Fertility Rate = Average number of children a woman has in her lifetime

📌 3 Key Objectives

Immediate Objective
Address unmet needs — contraception, health infrastructure, reproductive & child healthcare
Medium-Term Objective
Bring TFR to replacement level by 2010
Long-Term Objective
Achieve stable population by 2045 consistent with sustainable economic growth & environment protection

4. Human Resource Planning (HRP)

📌 Definition & 4 Major Activities

HRP = Strategy for Acquisition, Utilization, Development & Retention of human resources
Objective: Right people for right work + optimum utilization
Activity 1

Activity 2

Activity 3

📌 Need for HRP — 7 Reasons

  • Employment-Unemployment: Educated unemployed rising YET skill shortages exist
  • Technological Changes: Redundancies, retraining, redeployment needed
  • Organizational Changes: Cyclical fluctuations affect manpower needs
  • Demographic Changes: Age, sex, literacy profile shifts of workforce
  • Skill Shortages: Complex orgs need rare specialist skills
  • Governmental Influences: Affirmative action, labor laws, women/child employment restrictions
  • Pressure Groups: Unions & politicians demand internal recruitment preferences

5. Unemployment

📌 Definition & Formulas

Unemployed person = Active labour force member who SEEKS work but CANNOT find it
Number Unemployed = Labour Force − Work Force
Work Force = People working / engaged in economic activity
Labour Force = Work Force + Those seeking / available for work

📌 8 Types of Unemployment

Structural
Skill mismatch
Due to technology/demand change (e.g., computers replaced old workers)
Cyclical
Demand Deficient Unemployment
Economy-wide ↓ aggregate demand
Frictional
Temporary Unemployment
Transition period between jobs
Seasonal
Season-specific
e.g., non-cultivation period in agriculture
Natural Rate
Frictional + Structural
The baseline unavoidable unemployment
Open
Voluntary / Involuntary exclusion
Person excludes self from certain jobs
Disguised
Zero marginal productivity
More people employed than needed
Underemployment
Under-utilization
Skills / qualifications / hours not fully utilized
Natural Rate of Unemployment = Frictional + Structural — the irreducible minimum even in a healthy economy

📌 Nature in India — Rural vs Urban

Rural (3 Types):
1. Seasonal — agriculture-linked
2. Disguised — no training for non-agri
3. Chronic — workforce grows, jobs don't
Urban (3 Features):
1. Educated unemployed — flawed education system
2. Under-employment — irregular work
3. Jobs don't match qualifications → underpaid

5 Problems of Unemployment

Problem 1
Poverty
Problem 2
Under-utilization of human resources
Problem 3
Political instability — youth discontent
Problem 4
Social evils — drugs, theft, gambling
Problem 5
Economic exploitation — lower wages

7. Demand Estimate of Skilled Manpower

📌 Projections vs Estimates vs Forecasts

Projections
Outcome of spontaneous forces — no external stimulus assumed
Estimates
Educated guesses based on experience and available data
Forecasts
Predictions when normal events are influenced by external forces

📌 5 Types of Manpower Forecasts

Short-Term (≤ 2 years)
Micro-level | Financial provisions for wages | e.g., tourism events
Medium-Term (2–5 years)
Contingency planning | Policy advisory offices
Long-Term (> 5 years)
Macro-level | Educational planning for highly skilled professionals; corporate plans with tech changes
Policy Conditional
Based on rule-of-thumb / professional judgement / specified model; determined by policy towards manpower-influencing factors
Time Series Analysis
Statistical modeling of historical data to predict future manpower needs

📌 Trend Analysis — Worked Example

Given: Production = 5,000 units | Workers = 100 | Ratio = 100 ÷ 5,000 = 0.02
Estimated Production: 8,000 units → Workers needed = 8,000 × 0.02 = 160 workers

8. Migration

📌 Push & Pull Factors

PUSH Factors (Origin Region):
Unemployment | Poor conditions | Political turmoil | Natural disasters | Epidemics | Socio-economic backwardness
PULL Factors (Destination Region):
Better jobs | Better living conditions | Peace & stability | Security | Pleasant climate
  • Migration affects: Distribution & Density of population
  • Two regions: Donor Region (source) & Recipient Region (destination)

📌 Impacts on Source & Destination

Donor Region Effects

  • Under-utilization of housing, water, transport infrastructure
  • Example: Kerala men emigrate → higher sex ratio (1084)
  • Children + elderly > young adults in population

Recipient Region Effects

  • Pressure on housing, water, transport → SLUMS
  • ↑ Economic inequality | ↑ Crime rates
  • Public facilities badly affected

9. Rural Settlements & Economy

📌 4 Types of Rural Settlements

Type 1 · Clustered / Nucleated
Compact, closely built; living area distinct from farms & pastures
Type 2 · Semi-Clustered / Fragmented
Restricted clustering OR fragmentation of large village; caused by social segregation
Type 3 · Hamleted
Multiple units with common name — panna, para, palli, nagla, dhani; motivated by social/ethnic factors
Type 4 · Dispersed / Isolated
Isolated huts in jungles/hills; found in Meghalaya, Uttarakhand, HP, Kerala

📌 Features of Rural Economy

  • Agriculture-centric economy
  • Dominance of informal sector
  • Presence of small-scale and traditional industries
  • High dependency on monsoon
  • Seasonal unemployment & migration
  • Emerging rural non-farm sector

10. Sustainable Development of Cities

📌 Definition & Requirements

Sustainable urban development = Balancing social, economic & ecological needs of present AND future generations
  • Reviving growth | Changing quality of growth
  • Meeting essential needs: jobs, food, energy, water, sanitation
  • Ensuring sustainable population level
  • Conserving and enhancing resource base
  • Re-orientating technology and managing risk
  • Merging environment and economics in decision-making

📌 4 Pillars of Sustainable Development

🤝 Social Pillar
Education & health | Food & nutrition | Green housing | Water & sanitation | Green transport | Green energy | Recreation
💰 Economic Pillar
Green productive growth | Decent employment | Renewable energy production | Technology & R&D innovation
🏛️ Urban Governance Pillar
Planning & decentralization | Reduce inequities | Civil & political rights | Local-national-global links
🌿 Environmental Pillar
Forest & soil mgmt | Waste & recycling | Energy efficiency | Water mgmt | Air quality | Climate adaptation
